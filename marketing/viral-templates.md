# Viral Content Template Library

Use these templates when creating social media posts for GigForge, TechUni, or AI Elevate.
Each template has a psychological trigger, a fill-in formula, and platform-specific variants.

Pick 1-2 templates per post. Never use the same template twice in a row.

---

## 1. The Sacrifice Hook
**Psychology:** Reciprocity — "I did the hard work so you get the shortcut"
**Trigger:** Gratitude, obligation to engage

**Template:** `I spent [time] doing [hard thing] so you don't have to. Here's what I learned:`

**GigForge:** "I spent 3 weeks building a multi-agent AI system from scratch so you don't have to. Here's the architecture that actually works in production:"
**TechUni:** "I spent 200 hours testing every LMS on the market so you don't have to. Here's why we built our own:"

**LinkedIn:** Full story format, 3-5 key takeaways, end with "Save this for later"
**X/Twitter:** Punchy opener → thread with numbered lessons
**Bluesky:** Conversational tone, invite discussion

---

## 2. The Pattern Interrupt
**Psychology:** Cognitive dissonance — challenges what people assume is true
**Trigger:** Curiosity, need to resolve the conflict

**Template:** `Stop doing [common practice]. Do [better alternative] instead.`

**GigForge:** "Stop hiring agencies that bill by the hour. Hire one that bills by the deliverable."
**TechUni:** "Stop recording 45-minute lecture videos. Your students check out after 6 minutes."

**LinkedIn:** Bold statement → explain why → show data/proof → CTA
**X/Twitter:** One-liner, no thread needed. Let the replies do the engagement.
**Bluesky:** Add "hot take:" prefix, lean into the debate

---

## 3. The Contrast Frame
**Psychology:** Anchoring — the comparison makes the value obvious
**Trigger:** Perceived value, aspiration

**Template:** `The difference between a [low outcome] and a [high outcome] is [one specific thing].`

**GigForge:** "The difference between a $500 website and a $50K platform is architecture decisions made in week 1."
**TechUni:** "The difference between a course nobody finishes and one with 94% completion is interactive labs."

**LinkedIn:** Open with the contrast → tell a story about a specific project → reveal the insight
**X/Twitter:** Just the one-liner. Screenshot-worthy.
**Bluesky:** Add your own experience as proof

---

## 4. The Insider Secret
**Psychology:** Exclusivity — people want access to hidden knowledge
**Trigger:** FOMO, curiosity

**Template:** `Nobody talks about this, but [insider truth about industry].`

**GigForge:** "Nobody talks about this, but 80% of MVP failures aren't technical. They're scope failures in week 2."
**TechUni:** "Nobody talks about this, but most corporate training platforms charge you per seat AND per course. We don't."

**LinkedIn:** "I've worked with [N] clients and here's what I've noticed that nobody discusses..."
**X/Twitter:** Drop the truth bomb, no preamble
**Bluesky:** Frame as observation, invite others to share theirs

---

## 5. The Build-in-Public
**Psychology:** Transparency creates trust — people root for builders they can see
**Trigger:** Authenticity, parasocial investment

**Template:** `I built [thing] in [time]. Here's everything that went wrong (and right):`

**GigForge:** "We built a 50-agent AI system in 3 weeks. Here's the honest timeline — including the cryptominer we found on day 12:"
**TechUni:** "We launched our course platform with 22 microservices. Here's what broke on day 1:"

**LinkedIn:** Vulnerability + competence. Show the mess, then show the fix.
**X/Twitter:** Thread format. Real screenshots if possible.
**Bluesky:** Community loves this format. Be genuine.

---

## 6. The David vs Goliath
**Psychology:** Underdog effect — people instinctively root for the smaller player
**Trigger:** Tribal loyalty, anti-establishment

**Template:** `[Big competitor] charges $[X] for this. We built it for $[Y]. Here's how:`

**GigForge:** "Toptal charges $150/hr for a senior dev. We deliver the same quality at fixed price with AI-assisted development. Here's how:"
**TechUni:** "Teachable charges $99/month and you still can't run Docker labs. We do it for €49. Here's what you get:"

**LinkedIn:** Professional comparison, feature matrix, no trash-talking
**X/Twitter:** Provocative framing, invite the comparison
**Bluesky:** Focus on what you built, not what they charge

---

## 7. The Regret Avoidance
**Psychology:** Loss aversion — people fear missing out more than they desire gaining
**Trigger:** Urgency, self-protection

**Template:** `[N] things I wish I knew before [common action that your audience is about to do].`

**GigForge:** "5 things I wish I knew before hiring a dev agency for my MVP."
**TechUni:** "3 things I wish I knew before choosing an LMS for my company."

**LinkedIn:** Listicle format, each point is a mini-story
**X/Twitter:** Thread with one tweet per regret
**Bluesky:** Conversational, ask "what would you add?"

---

## 8. The Surprising Number
**Psychology:** Specificity creates credibility — vague claims feel fake, precise ones feel real
**Trigger:** Curiosity about the "how"

**Template:** `We [specific achievement with exact number]. Here's the [unexpected method]:`

**GigForge:** "We process 7 emails per day through 50 AI agents with zero manual intervention. Here's the workflow:"
**TechUni:** "Our platform runs 22 Docker containers on a single server for €55/month. Here's the architecture:"

**LinkedIn:** Lead with the number → break down the system → link to demo
**X/Twitter:** Number in first line. Thread the breakdown.
**Bluesky:** Numbers + "AMA" invitation

---

## 9. The Before/After
**Psychology:** Transformation narrative — the clearest way to show value
**Trigger:** Aspiration, "I want that result"

**Template:** `Before: [pain state]. After [our solution]: [result state].`

**GigForge:** "Before: 4 hours per day manually checking freelance platforms for leads. After our AI scout: 20 qualified leads delivered to your inbox daily."
**TechUni:** "Before: 3 months to build a training program. After AI course generation: 3 days."

**LinkedIn:** Side-by-side format, screenshots if available
**X/Twitter:** Two-line format. Before → After. Done.
**Bluesky:** Add "the tool that made the difference was..."

---

## 10. The Unpopular Opinion
**Psychology:** Social proof inversion — going against the crowd signals confidence
**Trigger:** Debate, strong reactions (engagement)

**Template:** `Unpopular opinion: [contrarian take on industry norm].`

**GigForge:** "Unpopular opinion: microservices are wrong for 90% of MVPs. Start with a monolith."
**TechUni:** "Unpopular opinion: video lectures are the worst way to teach technical skills. Hands-on labs beat them every time."

**LinkedIn:** State opinion → defend with evidence → invite disagreement
**X/Twitter:** Just the take. Let the quote tweets roll.
**Bluesky:** "I know this will be controversial, but..."

---

## 11. The How-To Thread
**Psychology:** Utility — actionable content gets saved and shared
**Trigger:** Practical value, bookmarking

**Template:** `How to [achieve desirable outcome] in [specific timeframe] (step-by-step):`

**GigForge:** "How to go from idea to deployed MVP in 14 days (step-by-step):"
**TechUni:** "How to create an AI-generated course in 30 minutes (step-by-step):"

**LinkedIn:** Numbered steps, each with a brief explanation
**X/Twitter:** Thread. One step per tweet. First tweet is the hook.
**Bluesky:** Same as X but more conversational

---

## 12. The Social Proof Stack
**Psychology:** Bandwagon effect — if others trust it, it must be good
**Trigger:** Safety in numbers, reduced risk

**Template:** `[N] [type of people] are already using [product] to [outcome]. Here's why:`

**GigForge:** "We've shipped 15 projects across 5 industries in the last quarter. Here's what they all had in common:"
**TechUni:** "200+ courses created on our platform. Here's what the most successful creators do differently:"

**LinkedIn:** Case study format, name industries, show results
**X/Twitter:** Lead with the number, thread the proof
**Bluesky:** Community-focused, highlight users

---

## 13. The Myth Buster
**Psychology:** Correcting false beliefs feels valuable — people share "TIL" moments
**Trigger:** Surprise, desire to share with others

**Template:** `"[Common belief]" — this is wrong. Here's what actually happens:`

**GigForge:** "'You need $100K to build a SaaS platform' — this is wrong. Here's what it actually costs:"
**TechUni:** "'AI will replace teachers' — this is wrong. Here's what AI actually does in education:"

**LinkedIn:** Myth → truth → evidence → what to do instead
**X/Twitter:** Myth in quotes → "Wrong." → Thread with truth
**Bluesky:** Educational tone, invite other myths to bust

---

## 14. The Tool Stack Reveal
**Psychology:** Utility + insider access — people love knowing what tools experts use
**Trigger:** Practical value, aspiration

**Template:** `My exact [tool/stack/setup] for [achieving outcome]:`

**GigForge:** "The exact tech stack we use to run 50+ AI agents in production (with costs):"
**TechUni:** "The exact stack behind our course platform — 22 services, €55/month total:"

**LinkedIn:** Detailed breakdown with links, costs, and why each choice
**X/Twitter:** Thread with one tool per tweet
**Bluesky:** Community loves tool reveals. Be specific.

---

## 15. The Challenge/Dare
**Psychology:** Ego engagement — people can't resist proving themselves
**Trigger:** Competition, self-assessment

**Template:** `I bet you can't [do specific thing] in [time constraint]. Prove me wrong.`

**GigForge:** "I bet you can't deploy a production-ready SaaS in under a week. We did it. Here's the proof."
**TechUni:** "I bet your training completion rate is under 40%. Here's how to fix that."

**LinkedIn:** Challenge → share your own result → invite others to share
**X/Twitter:** Direct challenge. Tag competitors if you're feeling bold.
**Bluesky:** Community challenge format, collaborative not combative

---

## Usage Rules for Social Agents

1. **Rotate templates** — never use the same template type two posts in a row
2. **Platform-adapt** — LinkedIn gets the long version, X gets the punchy version, Bluesky gets the community version
3. **Mix ratios** — 40% educational (#11, #14), 30% showcase (#5, #8, #9), 20% engagement (#2, #10, #15), 10% promotional (#6, #12)
4. **Always end with engagement** — ask a question, invite a reply, or prompt a save
5. **Use real numbers** — "50 agents" not "many agents", "€55/month" not "affordable"
6. **No AI language** — never say "leverage", "utilize", "cutting-edge", "game-changing", "revolutionize"
7. **One hook per post** — don't stack templates. Pick one, commit to it.
8. **Test and iterate** — track which templates get the most engagement per platform
